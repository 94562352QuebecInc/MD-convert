# SECTION 0 — INVESTMENT PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
<!-- SUMMARY-START -->

Apis Growth Fund II L.P. delivered strong performance through Q4 2024, achieving a gross total return of US$1.3B on US$499M invested, resulting in a 2.7x gross MoIC and 52% gross IRR since inception. The fund has returned US$343.5M in proceeds to date, yielding a DPI of 0.9x—a strong liquidity profile for a growth equity fund still holding significant unrealized value.

The portfolio benefited from continued tailwinds in digital adoption and financial inclusion across Africa and Asia. TymeBank emerged as a standout performer, achieving unicorn status following a US$250M Series D led by Nubank at a US$1.2B pre-money valuation. With 14.4M customers and 21% LTM NOI growth, TymeBank represents a meaningful portion of fund value. Coda Payments remains the top performer at 14.2x MoIC and 183% IRR, though its valuation declined QoQ from US$389.6M to US$396.1M as the company refocused operations following publisher delays.

The fund's NAV declined 1.6% QoQ to US$919.4M, driven primarily by unrealized losses of US$43M—largely concentrated in Tutuka (down US$28M) and Chrysanthemum/Coda (down US$70M combined). These markdowns offset gains in Clover/Cashfree (+US$36M) and Tiger Lily/TymeBank (+US$26M).

With the investment period ended in October 2023, the fund maintains adequate dry powder (US$126.7M unfunded) for follow-on investments through March 2027. The portfolio is well-diversified across 13 investments spanning payments, lending, insurance, and digital banking in India, South Africa, Indonesia, Egypt, and Singapore.

Key monitoring items include: Giift's negative EBITDA trajectory, Money View's slight NAV decline, and DTOne's flat performance at 1.0x. Hero FinCorp's pending IPO and RenewBuy's strategic M&A exploration represent near-term catalysts. Overall, the fund demonstrates strong value creation with meaningful realizations and continued upside potential in its core holdings.

## Company overview

- The Company is connected to 900+ operators and billers across 180 countries, processing over 110 million transactions annually in over 100 currencies for more than 75 million unique recipients. Its clients are primarily MNOs and cross-border money remittance firms who provide their clients with the ability to purchase airtime credit across borders.
- DTOne was previously known as TransferTo prior to the split of TransferTo into two companies DTOne and Thunes (a cross-border money remittance firm) in 2018.

## Investment thesis

- *Market leader:* DTOne is the #2 player in the US$ 2B (McKinsey estimate) market for cross-border airtime transfers, and is closely behind the #1 player. - *Multiple Growth Levers:* (i) The low fixed transfer costs of airtime transfers combined with DTOne's wide geographic footprint have allowed DTOne to explore the potential of micropayments with social media for customer acquisition and retention (ii) DTOne's platform also enables domestic airtime sales which has allowed the company to develop a significant domestic airtime distribution business. (iii) The strong EBITDA generation coupled with attractive M&A targets in similar or adjacent MNO-focused market segments creates room also for potential acquisitions.

## Quarterly update

- *Transactional:* The Apis and DTOne teams are currently in M&A discussions with several potential parties.

## 14. Commitments and contingent liabilities

On 6 December 2022, the Partnership, together with Apis Growth II (B) L.P. entered into a Bank Guarantee with Investec Bank Limited in relation to a deferred cash payment due to shareholders of one of Apis Growth Fund II L.P.'s investments – TymeBank. The Partnership's share of this guarantee amounts to USD 11,208,082 (2023: USD 11,208,082).

On 30 October 2023, the guarantee provided by the Partnership was reduced to USD 4,818,424.

On 26 July 2024, the Partnership, together with Apis Growth II (B) L.P., renewed the Bank Guaranteed amount with Investec Bank Limited which expired on 31 January 2025.

In relation to the Guaranteed amounts mentioned above, the Partnership incurs a 'Bank Guarantee fee rate' of 1.7% per annum payable to Investec Bank Limited. The fee for the year amounted to USD 80,893 (2023: USD 161,244).

The Partnership charges a fee to TymeBank for the provision of the guarantee. This is determined at a rate of 15.0% per annum on the amount of the guarantee provided. The fee for the year amounted to USD 722,764 (2023: USD 1,635,539).
