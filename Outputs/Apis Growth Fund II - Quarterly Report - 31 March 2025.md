# SECTION 0 — INVESTMENT PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
<!-- SUMMARY-START -->

Apis Growth Fund II L.P. delivered a challenging Q1 2025, with portfolio NAV declining 7.5% quarter-over-quarter from $919.4M to $855.4M, driven by $66.5M in unrealized losses across the portfolio. Despite the quarterly drawdown, the fund maintains strong long-term performance metrics with a 2.5x gross MoIC and 47% IRR on $502.5M of total invested capital since inception.

The portfolio demonstrates significant concentration risk, with top performers Coda Payments (13.8x MoIC, 182% IRR) and Tutuka (3.9x MoIC, 57% IRR) representing substantial value creation. However, several holdings experienced material writedowns this quarter, including MNT-Halan (-$17.3M unrealized loss), Coda (-$12.4M), Hero FinCorp (-$9.5M), and Giift (-$9.4M). The MNT-Halan decline is particularly notable given its Egypt exposure and currency pressures.

From a liquidity perspective, the fund has generated $343.5M in cumulative realizations, producing a 0.9x DPI—indicating strong progress toward capital return. The fund maintains $123.9M in dry powder (unfunded commitments minus recallable distributions) for follow-on investments, which is permitted until March 2027.

Portfolio company operational highlights are mixed: TymeBank shows strong momentum with 16M+ customers and 21% YoY NOI growth, while RenewBuy and Ezra demonstrate healthy traction. However, Hero FinCorp's asset quality deterioration (GNPA rising to 5.4%, PAT down 70% YoY) warrants monitoring, and Coda's monthly burn reduction from $2.0M to $0.7M suggests operational challenges.

Key transactional developments include Cashfree's $23M primary round at a $644M valuation (including $3M from the Fund), TymeBank's Series D completion with a Nu Bank call option, and potential M&A discussions at Doku and DTOne. The investment period ended in October 2023, shifting focus to portfolio optimization and exits.

## FUND MANAGER'S REPORT

We are pleased to share the quarterly report for Apis Growth Fund II L.P. (together with its parallel partnership, Apis Growth Fund II (B) L.P. the "Fund") for the quarter ended 31 March 2025 (Q1 2025).

The global economy is at a critical juncture: major policy shifts are poised to reset the international trade system, giving rise to uncertainty.
